# Can Low-Dimensional Light Illuminate a High-Dimensional World?

> From Information Compression and Sensory Dimensionality Reduction to Cognitive Dimensionality Ascent — On the Boundaries of Text-Based Training and the Evolutionary Trajectory of Intelligence

---

**Abstract**: Large language models (LLMs), trained on the entirety of human textual output, exhibit astonishing intelligence. Yet text itself is a high-rate lossy compression of the real world — it discards the raw information of sensory dimensions such as vision, hearing, touch, and smell, collapsing the infinite-dimensional real into finite sequences of symbols. Drawing on information theory, the symbol grounding problem, embodied cognition philosophy, and current AI frontier practice, this article argues for a core thesis: **low-dimensional cognition (text-based training) cannot autonomously evolve into high-dimensional cognition (real-world understanding); dimensionality ascent necessarily depends on the injection of new information sources.** The "intelligence" of LLMs is, in essence, a compressed replica of collective human intelligence — an efficient tool, not a genuine agent in the full sense. The path toward high-dimensional perceptual-cognitive intelligence lies in embodied training within the real world and the construction of world models.

## 1. All Cognition Begins with the Senses: How Information Flows from the World to Intelligence

> "If you describe three-dimensional space to a two-dimensional being, it will be bewildered. By the same logic, a language model trained solely on text, even when told what the real world is like, cannot generate effective understanding."
> — The core intuition of this article

### The Senses Are Intelligence's Information Gateway

Human and animal cognition does not arise from nothing. Vision, hearing, touch, smell — these sensory channels are the natural input ports for our training data. Infants build intuitions of object permanence through touch, learn spatial relationships through vision, and capture rhythm and emotion through hearing. These sensory data streams, shaped by hundreds of millions of years of evolutionary selection, have formed an efficient system of world encoding — neural circuitry — the hardware foundation of our a priori cognition.

The deep learning pioneered by Geoffrey Hinton, 2024 Nobel laureate in Physics, is fundamentally a computational approximation of biological neural networks. But biological neural networks possess one critical advantage: **they receive raw signals directly from the physical world**, not secondary products filtered through human symbolization.

### Missing Input, Collapsed Structure

A frequently underestimated piece of evidence comes from developmental neuroscience: the case of congenitally deaf individuals. "Deafness precedes muteness" — the absence of auditory input does not directly damage the vocal apparatus; rather, the feedback loop of the auditory cortex cannot be established, causing a systemic failure of the language capacity chain. The classic experiments of Hubel and Wiesel (1965) further demonstrated that during the critical period of visual development, occluding one eye leads to a permanent reorganization of neuronal connections in the corresponding visual cortex — even when the ocular structure remains intact.

**The quality and dimensionality of input determine the structural ceiling of a cognitive system.** This is not philosophical deduction but hard neuroscientific fact.

> Key corollary: If an agent is "trained" exclusively through text — a form of information already highly compressed by human intelligence — the upper bound of its cognitive dimensionality will forever be constrained by the information dimensionality that text can carry.

## 2. The Birth of Language: Intelligence's First Compression of the World

In the evolutionary history of humanity, the emergence of language marks a watershed. It enabled individual experience to be externalized, shared, and transmitted across generations — the very foundation of civilization. Yet from an information-theoretic perspective, language is also **human intelligence's first large-scale lossy compression of the real world**.

### From Infinite Dimensions to Discrete Symbols

The real world is infinite-dimensional: the color of an apple (a continuous spectral distribution), its tactile feel (continuous variation in temperature and texture), its scent (a combination of hundreds of volatile molecules), its weight (the actual sensation of mass) — all these parallel sensory properties collapse in language into two characters: "苹果" (apple).

Shannon (1948), in his foundational work on information theory, already made clear: any encoding process is a compression of the source, and lossy compression means **the original information cannot be uniquely recovered from the compressed signal**. You cannot reconstruct a real apple from the word "apple" — its color, weight, scent, texture, and even its specific variety are all irretrievably lost.

### Ted Chiang's JPEG Metaphor

In 2023, science fiction writer Ted Chiang published an influential essay in *The New Yorker* with a resonant title: **"ChatGPT Is a Blurry JPEG of the Web."** The core metaphor is strikingly precise:

- Internet text is a **lossy compression output** of humanity's interaction with the world
- LLMs perform a **further compression** of this already lossy output
- The generation process is **interpolative reconstruction** — it appears coherent and plausible, but the details are unreliable
- "Hallucination" is not a bug but an **inevitable product of lossy compression** — just as JPEG compression introduces artifacts

Chiang's metaphor aligns closely with the core intuition of this article: **information flows from the world to language in a unidirectional, lossy manner. Any attempt to reverse-engineer the world from language can, at best, yield only a blurry reconstruction.**

### Compression as Intelligence? The Hutter Prize and the Inverse Perspective

The Human Knowledge Compression Prize (Hutter Prize), established by Marcus Hutter, defines "intelligence" as "compression capacity" — whoever can encode the greatest amount of human knowledge in the smallest file is the most intelligent. From this angle, LLMs are indeed the strongest "compressors" to date — they "compress" the entirety of internet text into a few hundred gigabytes of weight files.

But a fundamental distinction exists between "compression-intelligence" and "comprehension-intelligence." A JPEG compression algorithm does not know whether a photograph contains a person or a landscape — it merely encodes statistical regularities efficiently. By the same token, does the efficient compression achieved by LLMs imply that they "understand" the semantics of the compressed content? This is precisely the core question raised by Searle's Chinese Room argument.

## 3. The Price of Compression: Why Text Can Never Reconstruct Reality

### The Unidirectional Causal Chain

World → Sensory Perception → Intelligent Understanding → Linguistic Expression

This is a unidirectional, irreversible causal chain. Each arrow signifies **information loss and dimensionality reduction**:

1. **World → Senses**: What you see, hear, and smell is not the totality of the world's facts. The visible spectrum constitutes only a narrow band of the electromagnetic spectrum; the human ear detects only frequencies between 20 Hz and 20 kHz. **Human sensation itself is already a dimensionality reduction.**

2. **Senses → Intelligence**: Sensory input passes through attentional filtering, memory selection, cognitive bias, and experiential interpretation — vast amounts of raw information are discarded, retaining only features relevant to survival and adaptation. **This layer of dimensionality reduction is shaped by evolutionary pressure.**

3. **Intelligence → Language**: Continuous sensations, intuitions, emotions, and bodily feelings must be "translated" into discrete sequences of symbols. From "I feel a warmth, with a certain soft tactile quality, and the scent in the air reminds me of a childhood afternoon" to "The weather is nice today" — **this is the most extreme layer of compression.**

### The Impossibility of Swimming Upstream

The training process of LLMs is, in essence, an attempt to reverse-engineer from the terminal end of the chain (language) back to the origin (world). This is precisely the **fundamental difficulty** they face:

- The sensory information lost by language cannot be recovered through any statistical method
- Different speakers possess different prior experiences (personal history, cultural background), and the linguistic encoding does not contain all the "keys" needed for decompression
- Even with the sum total of all human text, one possesses only a **projection** of the world, not the world itself

This explains why LLMs, even when excelling at text-based tasks, still fail frequently on physical reasoning, spatial navigation, and causal inference that require real-world common sense — because text simply does not contain all the information required to solve these problems.

## 4. Prior Anchoring: Why Scientific Texts Converge and Literary Texts Diverge

### The Composition of Cognition

> Textual information + Prior information = Text-level cognition

This is a concise yet powerful cognitive formula. The same passage of text produces different understandings in different readers because their "prior information" differs. But when prior information is anchored to a shared public fact, understanding is anchored along with it.

### Two Case Studies

**Scientific text**: Faced with a theorem of physics, every trained physicist in the world who reads it arrives at the same understanding. This is because their prior information is anchored to the same formal system (mathematical axioms, experimental methods). Convergent priors → convergent cognition.

**Literary text**: Faced with a line of poetry — "A solitary column of smoke rises straight over the vast desert; the long river reflects the setting sun, perfectly round" — a person nursing a recent heartbreak, a herdsman who grew up on the northwestern plains, and a student from the south who has never set eyes on a desert will experience entirely different feelings. Divergent priors → divergent cognition.

### Implications for LLMs

LLMs perform far better on scientific Q&A than on subjective judgment tasks — not because they are "better at science," but because the prior consistency among scientific texts in the training corpus is far higher than that among literary or subjective texts. The same physical formula is expressed with high convergence across all human texts; the same emotional experience appears in countless divergent forms across different people's writing.

**This further supports the "language as compressed output" thesis: when the compressed sources (priors) are inconsistent, a single compressed output (text) can correspond to multiple different decompression results.**

## 5. The Dimensionality Cage: Where Are the Upper Limits of Text-Based Models?

### Two-Dimensional Beings and a Three-Dimensional World

> Describe three-dimensional space to a two-dimensional being, and it cannot understand. By the same logic, a language model trained on text, even when told what the real world is like, cannot generate effective understanding.

The logical kernel of this analogy: **the dimensionality ceiling of a cognitive system is determined by the dimensionality of its training data.** A two-dimensional being's entire perceptual space is two-dimensional; it possesses no "organ" — whether physical or cognitive — for receiving three-dimensional information.

An LLM's entire "perceptual space" consists of text sequences. Text is one-dimensional (a stream of symbols along the time axis). Even if the "semantic dimensionality" of textual content can be high, the **data format itself that the model processes is one-dimensional**. To understand three-dimensional space, four-dimensional spacetime, or higher-dimensional abstract structures by reverse-engineering from one-dimensional sequences is information-theoretically infeasible — lost information cannot be recovered, only interpolatively filled in.

### Othello-GPT: A Counterexample That Must Be Taken Seriously

The 2023 ICLR paper on Othello-GPT (Li et al.) presented a finding that warrants serious consideration: a language model trained purely on text sequences (only chess-move notation as input) exhibited internal neuronal activation patterns that **linearly represented the world state of the board** — which squares held black pieces, which held white pieces. Researchers could even alter the model's predictive outputs through causal interventions on these representations.

Does this refute the "dimensionality cage" hypothesis?

**Partially.** Othello-GPT demonstrates that text sequences can indeed encode structural information beyond the surface level, and that this encoding is linear and intervenable. But it does not fully overturn the core thesis of this article — because:

1. **The Othello world is of minimal dimensionality** (an 8×8 board with 3 states), whereas the real world is high-dimensional, continuous, and dynamic
2. **Othello training is single-domain** (only game records), whereas LLMs face text from all domains
3. **Discovered representations ≠ full understanding** — Othello-GPT's board representations serve only to predict moves and cannot generalize to higher-dimensional cognition such as "strategic reasoning" or "aesthetic judgment"

A more appropriate analogy may be: a system trained in a 2D world can indeed learn certain regularities of "3D projection" (just as we, standing on the ground, can infer an object's 3D shape from its shadow), but it can never truly "enter" 3D space to reason. The same holds for LLMs — the world knowledge they acquire is a projection of the world, not the world itself.

### Diminishing Marginal Returns of Scaling: The Industry Is Hitting a Wall

At the practical level, multiple signals indicate that the dimensionality ceiling of text pretraining is becoming apparent:

- **Ilya Sutskever (OpenAI co-founder)** stated publicly at NeurIPS 2024: "The era of pretraining as we know it is coming to an end. Internet data is the fossil fuel of AI — it will eventually run out."
- **The power-law nature of Scaling Laws** (Kaplan et al., 2020) means that each 10× increase in compute yields only a predictable, limited improvement — you are already paying exponential costs for logarithmic gains
- **The repeated delays of GPT-5** and the industry-wide pivot toward reasoning-time compute (o1/o3-type models) rather than continued scaling of pretraining suggest that pretraining scaling has reached its practical boundary
- **Yann LeCun** has publicly and persistently argued for years that the pure-text LLM path cannot lead to human-level AI; "word models" can never substitute for "world models." His AMI Labs, backed by a $1.03 billion seed round (March 2025), is now fully committed to building world models

## 6. The Denominator Effect: Why LLMs Appear So Intelligent

If LLMs truly face such fundamental dimensional constraints, why do they still exhibit impressive intelligence across so many tasks?

### Collective Human Intelligence Compressed vs. Individual Cognition

The answer lies in the **sheer size of the denominator**.

LLMs' training corpora encompass the distilled textual essence of thousands of years of human civilization — all scientific papers, all philosophical works, all legal documents, all code repositories, all literary creations, all technical manuals, all encyclopedia entries. Even if 99.9% of the information is lost across successive layers of compression, the remaining 0.1% of the collective intelligence of humanity is still sufficient to overwhelm any single human individual along a single dimension.

- No single human can be proficient in all programming languages → An LLM can
- No single human can memorize all major legal systems → An LLM can
- No single human can comprehend all scientific domains → An LLM can

This is not because LLMs are a higher form of intelligence than humans, but because **an LLM is one entity versus the entire collective of humanity — an inherently asymmetric comparison**, fundamentally unfair.

### "Super-Tool," Not "Super-Intelligence"

From this perspective, LLMs belong in the same category as steam engines, automobiles, and computers — they are **products and amplifiers of human intelligence**, efficiency-enhancing tools. They do not "understand" thermodynamics — they merely (through human design) exploit thermodynamics. Likewise, LLMs do not "understand" language — they merely (through human annotation and data engineering) efficiently replicate the statistical regularities of human linguistic behavior.

Richard Sutton (Turing Award laureate, father of reinforcement learning) noted in 2025 that LLMs consistently fail at long-term planning and multi-step reasoning — even when they can produce a perfect planning document, they cannot genuinely execute and monitor an action chain exceeding five steps. This is because planning requires a "world model" — the capacity to simulate the consequences of actions in cognitive space — and LLMs lack the foundational substrate for this space.

### Moravec's Paradox

AI pioneer Hans Moravec identified this paradox as early as 1988:

> It is comparatively easy to make computers exhibit adult-level performance on intelligence tests or playing checkers, and difficult or impossible to give them the perceptual and motor skills of a one-year-old.

This time, LLMs perfectly confirm Moravec's prediction: high-level symbolic reasoning (akin to IQ tests) is an LLM strength — because this requires only text-level pattern matching; low-level sensorimotor coordination (grasping objects, navigating unfamiliar environments) is nearly impossible for LLMs — because this requires real-time perception-action closed loops in the physical world.

## 7. The Path of Dimensionality Ascent: Reverse Engineering the Sensory–World Model–Language Chain

If pure text training cannot produce genuine world understanding, then where does the path lie?

### Core Strategy: Traverse the Causal Chain in Reverse

Recall the unidirectional causal chain proposed by this article:

> World → Senses → Intelligence → Language

Pure text training attempts to jump directly from "language" to "intelligence," bypassing the two critical information sources: "senses" and "world." The correct path should **begin at the source — the world itself**:

**Step One: Establish the Sensory Layer**

Embodied robots — equipped with rich sensor arrays (cameras = vision, microphone arrays = hearing, tactile sensors = touch, torque sensors = proprioception) — engage in interactive training directly within the real world. This corresponds to the "sensorimotor stage" of human infant development (Piaget's theory), during which the agent, through extensive trial-and-error interaction with the physical environment, builds a foundational world model — object permanence, causality, spatial relations, force and motion.

**Step Two: Cross-Modal Mapping**

On the foundation of the sensory-world model, map existing linguistic symbols (concepts) onto the world representations acquired through sensors. This includes:
- **Conceptual mapping**: The word "apple" ⇄ visual images of apples ⇄ tactile sensations of apple hardness ⇄ gustatory sensations of apple sweetness and tartness
- **Logical mapping**: The sentence "if dropped, it will shatter" ⇄ statistical regularities from repeated observation or execution of dropping actions

This is precisely the orthodox solution that Stevan Harnad (1990) proposed for the Symbol Grounding Problem: through causal sensor-world connections, ground abstract symbols onto the physical entities and events of the real world. These grounded symbols can then be used to construct more complex language and reasoning — language is no longer a castle in the air.

**Step Three: World Model Feeds Back into Language Model**

Once the world model attains a certain level of intelligence, it can in turn train and correct the language model — perhaps even rendering a separate language model unnecessary from the start, because the world model itself would inherently encompass linguistic comprehension and reasoning capabilities (just as human cognition spans a continuous spectrum from perception to language, rather than comprising two separate modules).

### The Industry Is Already Moving: This Path Is No Longer Hypothetical

This trajectory is not merely theoretical deduction. Leading AI laboratories are actively executing it:

| Laboratory | Investment in World Models / Embodied AI | Signature Move |
|------------|------------------------------------------|----------------|
| **Meta / Yann LeCun** | Core strategy, $1.03 billion | AMI Labs (2025), dedicated to building AI that understands the physical world |
| **OpenAI** | Restarted robotics division | Secret robotics lab in San Francisco (2026), collecting human manipulation data |
| **Tesla** | Full-stack in-house humanoid robots | Optimus, thousands deployed in factories, acquiring training data from physical interaction |
| **Google DeepMind** | SIMA + robotics | Training general agents in virtual and physical environments |
| **Chinese Players** | Humanoid robot factories at scale | Multiple enterprises deploying humanoid robots in manufacturing settings |

### Key Insight: The Logical Structure of Cross-Dimensional Training

Returning to the titular question of this article: **how can low-dimensional cognitive training evolve into high-dimensional intelligence?**

The answer: **same-dimensional training cannot cross-dimensionally evolve into high-dimensional intelligence. Dimensionality ascent must proceed through the injection of high-dimensional information sources.** Language (a low-dimensional compressed output) does not itself contain all the information required for dimensionality ascent. But language can serve as a "scaffold" — once real-world sensory data is injected into the system, linguistic mapping can help the system:
1. Structurally organize sensory experiences
2. "Verbalize" tacit knowledge acquired in the physical world and use it for reasoning
3. Access the knowledge base accumulated by humanity across generations (scientific laws, engineering standards, ethical frameworks)

This aligns with the developmental trajectory of the human child: sensorimotor experience is the foundation; language is the edifice built upon it. You cannot construct a building in midair without a foundation — but once the foundation exists, the structure can far exceed the foundation's own limits.

## 8. Conclusion and Outlook

### The Core Framework of This Article

| Link | Relationship | Core Claim |
|------|-------------|------------|
| World → Senses | Dimensionality reduction | Senses are humanity's natural training data gateway, but information loss is already present |
| Senses → Intelligence | Compression + Organization | Attention, memory, and experience compress sensory data into usable prior knowledge |
| Intelligence → Language | High-rate compression | Language is irreversible, lossy symbolic compression; vast sensory dimensions are discarded |
| Language → LLM | Statistical fitting | LLMs learn the statistical regularities of the world's projection, not the world's causal structure |
| LLM "Intelligence" | Denominator effect | LLM "intelligence" is the statistical replica of compressed collective human intelligence |
| Dimensionality Ascent Path | Information source injection | Real-world sensory data + embodied interaction are required; dimensionality ascent cannot spontaneously emerge from low-dimensional data |

### Philosophical Stance

**LLMs are tools, not autonomous agents.** They belong to the same category as automobiles, trains, and computers — products and amplifiers of human intelligence. True intelligence should be based on observation, sensation, and understanding of the real world, not derived from text statistics alone.

This simultaneously addresses a key question in AI ethics: we need not fear LLMs "waking up" to produce superhuman intelligence — because the dimensionality ceiling of text-based training determines that they cannot generate understanding that transcends the scope of human cognition. **More efficient pattern matching within the same dimension is not a dimensionality-reduction strike from a higher dimension.**

### A Final Thought Experiment

> If the human visible spectrum extended into ultraviolet and infrared, if our hearing range reached into ultrasound, then —
> - What form would our painting take? (The ultraviolet floral patterns that bees see?)
> - What form would our music take? (Rhythms in the auditory frequency range of bats?)
> - What language would we use to describe the world, beauty, and truth?

This is not pure fantasy. The concept of the *Umwelt* (subjective perceptual world), proposed by German zoologist Jakob von Uexküll in the early twentieth century, already made clear: every living being inhabits its own unique perceptual universe, defined by the range of its senses. The bee's Umwelt contains ultraviolet floral patterns (invisible to humans); the bat's Umwelt is an acoustic space (inaudible to humans) — these "worlds" are as real as the human world, yet entirely different.

**Returning to AI: current LLMs inhabit the Umwelt of text. Their world has boundaries. To let them see the broader spectrum, we need to install new sensory channels — eyes, ears, skin, and bodies that can move. Then let them, like infants, start from zero: touch, try, fall, and stand up again.**

Low-dimensional light cannot illuminate a high-dimensional world — but it can serve as a navigational beacon, guiding us toward directions that have not yet been touched by the light.
